Prithviraj chauhan

Prithviraj Chauhan, also known as Rai Pithora, was a legendary Rajput king of the Chauhan dynasty who ruled from Ajmer and Delhi in the twelfth century CE. Renowned for his extraordinary valor and leadership, he unified many Rajput clans and bravely defended his kingdom against repeated invasions. His greatest legacy comes from the heroic battles of Tarain, where he fiercely resisted the advancing forces of Muhammad Ghori. Although he was eventually defeated in the Second Battle of Tarain in 1192, Prithviraj Chauhan remains celebrated in Indian history as a symbol of courage, patriotism, and unwavering spirit in the face of adversity.
